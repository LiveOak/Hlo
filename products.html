Consulting for Statistical Analysis and Software Development

Most services and software produced by Howard Live Oak have been develop for private clients. Discussing these products could reveal proprietary information, or provide insight into systems managing protected health information (PHI).  However the following products are available to the public.

NlsyLinks

 

Is an R package developed for the NIH grant "NLSY Kinship Links: Reliable and Valid Sibling Identification" (Joe Rodgers, PI). It provides utilities and kinship information for Behavior Genetics and Developmental research using the NLSY (National Longitudinal Survey of Youth). The release version and documentation is available on CRAN.  The development version is available on R-Forge.

BBAID: The Balance Beam Aid for Instruction in Diagnosis

 

Is a product developed for Rob Hamm, PhD, in the Family and Preventive Medicine department in the University of Oklahoma Health Sciences Center.  It graphically demonstrates a simplified process of differential diagnosis for teaching purposes.  Each diagnosis is represented by a side of a balance beam. As a clinician specifies the evidence (e.g., lab tests or patient symptoms), the diagram places a token on the beam to indicate the new center of gravity.  Currently it is implemented as a Windows Presentation Foundation (WPF) .NET GUI, deployed with ClickOnce technology; WPF was chosen because rendering tasks are offloaded to the GPU, which permits the balance beam elements to be quickly redrawn as a user hovers over different symptoms in the right panel.  Only Windows is currently supported, but we hope to receive additional grant funding to bring it on other platforms.  The primary download location is hosted by Family Medicine; in case that is temporarily unavailable, please use this backup location.
